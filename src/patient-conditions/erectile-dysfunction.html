Following radial prostatectomy, approximately 30% of patients have erectile dysfunction secondary to vascular and neurogenic causes. Although the cavernosal nerves may be preserved during a radical prostatectomy, a majority of men suffer from neuropraxia, or nerve paralysis, which may last from months to years. Nerve injury can independently lead to vascular injury and subsequent penile scarring. This can be a major cause of erectile dysfunction in men following radical prostatectomy.

The concept of erectile preservation is to increase blood flow and oxygen to the penile tissue and thus prevent scarring and permanent damage to the penis. It is important to take this proactive approach because many times the scarring that occurs in the penis after a radical prostatectomy is irreversible.

There are many medications that can be used to prevent penile scarring. Daily Cialis 5mg can be effective in preserving erectile function.
